Fluffy Delight – The Perfect Pancake Recipe

Pancakes are more than just a breakfast dish—they are a symbol of comfort and simplicity. Their soft, fluffy texture and warm golden color make them irresistible to both children and adults alike.

Every culture has its own version of pancakes, from crepes in France to the thick American stacks. Despite their variety, one thing remains the same: pancakes always bring joy to the table.

For me, pancakes represent togetherness. Whether it’s a weekend brunch with family or a cozy morning alone, a plate of pancakes sets the mood for a happy, relaxed start.

Ingredients and Preparation

To make classic pancakes, you will need: one cup of all-purpose flour, one tablespoon of sugar, one teaspoon of baking powder, a pinch of salt, one egg, three-fourths cup of milk, and one tablespoon of melted butter. These simple ingredients are the key to soft and fluffy results.

Begin by mixing the dry ingredients—flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt—in a bowl. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg, milk, and melted butter together until smooth.

Slowly combine the wet and dry mixtures, stirring gently to avoid overmixing. The batter should be thick but pourable. Let it rest for a few minutes to ensure the pancakes turn out airy and light.

Cooking and Serving

Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with butter. Pour a small ladle of batter onto the pan, letting it spread naturally into a circle.

Cook until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges begin to set, then flip carefully to cook the other side. Each pancake should turn golden brown with a soft, fluffy center.
